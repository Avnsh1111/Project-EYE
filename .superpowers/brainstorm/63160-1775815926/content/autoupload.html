Auto-Upload & Sync Behaviour

How should new photos/files get from devices to your self-hosted server?

A

Instant Background Sync (Google Photos style)

Mobile Desktop

As soon as new photos land on your camera roll (mobile) or watched folder (desktop), they queue up and upload automatically in the background — even when the app is closed. Upload over WiFi only or any connection (configurable).

Smart Scheduled Sync + Manual Upload

Mobile Desktop

Sync runs on a schedule (every 15 min / 1 hr / daily) or when you open the app. Manual upload always available. Less battery drain, simpler permissions. Desktop uses a tray app that syncs watched folders on schedule.

Hybrid — Instant on WiFi, Scheduled on Mobile Data

Mobile Desktop

Best of both: when on WiFi the app syncs instantly as photos arrive (like Google Photos). On mobile data it queues and syncs on schedule or when WiFi reconnects. Desktop app watches folders instantly. Manual upload always available with drag-and-drop on web.

Desktop Watch-Folder + Manual Upload on Mobile

Desktop only auto Mobile manual

Desktop app watches configured folders (Downloads, Pictures, screenshots) and auto-uploads anything new. On mobile, you choose what to upload — no background sync, no permissions needed. Good if the self-hosted server is on your home network only.

My recommendation: C — This is exactly how Google Photos works. WiFi = instant, mobile data = queued. It requires a background service and an offline queue but gives the most authentic experience. The NativePHP Mobile APIs (via Capacitor) give us the background task and filesystem access needed.
